Overview

Purpose

My goal for my website is to sell bicycles. My friend Adam buys used or almost new bicycles. He fixes them. Then he sells them to make extra money. He needs a website as a storefront to advertise and sell his bicycles. I want to build a website he can use to show pictures and information of his bikes. I want the website to also be a way the customers can contact him and ask questions about his bikes.

People who love biking

Bike Catalog

There will be pictures of all the bikes we are selling with their specifications and cost. We will answer their questions about different type of bikes and what type of bike would best for the type of activity of buyer wants. There will be pictures of all the bikes we are selling with their specifications and cost. We will arrange at time to come to look at our bikes. We have a large variety of bikes for all age groups. Types of bikes • Road Bikes—They have drop down handlebars and skinny tires. The handlebars are very light weight and aerodynamic. The frame is also very lightweight. The more expensive versions are made from carbon and cost several thousand dollars. Their uses are racing, touring, fitness riding, long-distance rides and daily commuting. Price is from $800 to $10,000. • Mountain bikes—are designed with excellent braking and shock-absorbing features which help them to handle the obstacles encountered on trails such as rocks, dirt trails, roots and ruts. They are also meant to deal with steeper terrain. They have knobby and wider tires which give them traction with rugged terrain. Prices from $700 to $6,000. • Touring bikes—are designed for long distance travel. They have attachment points which allow you to attach fenders, pumps, lights, racks, water bottles, saddle bags and so much more. Price $600 to $1,000. • Folding bikes—these bikes have a folding frame, handlebar and saddle and carried in a carry bag which allows the rider to fold them when they need them to take less space such as on a subway, bus, boat or in a home with limited storage space. Price $400 to 5,000 • BMX—Bicycle Motor Cross—are single speed bikes used to race around a dirt track with obstacles. They have one speed and 20-inch wheel. Price $400 to $700 • Recumbent bike—This a non-traditional bike. It is lower than a normal bike almost sitting on the ground. There are different configurations with long or short wheelbases and varieties of two, three or four wheelers as well as under and above seat steering. Price $1,500 to 2,500. • Cruiser—these bikes are meant of leisurely rides someone would take around the town or neighborhood. They sometimes have wider seats for better comfort and relaxed seating positions. Prices $400 to $1,000 • Hybrid Bike—This bike is a combination of road, mountain and touring bike designs. They are do-it-all kind of bikes and can be used for many different uses. Many are commuter-friendly and have fenders, racks and lighting systems. Since hybrid bikes have so many different uses it is important to know what biking requirements you have so you can have the best fit. Price $500 to $1,800 • Electric Bike—These bikes have an electric motor in them that can be charged at night. They are growing in popularity. There are mountain, road and hybrid versions available. They make biking easier, but still give a great workout. Price $1,500 to $2,000

Biking Adventures

We will have a page for the biking trips that we are offering from one day trips to multiple day trips and the locations. We also sponsor bike scenic bike trips from days trips to overnight trips. We also sponsor our annual Cincinnati to Cleveland trip on the historic Ohio-Eric Canal Towpath that takes about a week to complete. We will also have a free weekly bike ride on Saturday mornings on the Ohio/Erie Canal Towpath with different locations weekly. We will have a calendar that will show our different monthly bike tours from March to October. We will also be offering some century bike rides for riders who have riding 100 miles as a goal.
